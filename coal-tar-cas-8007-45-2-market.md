<p><p>Coal tar, a thick black liquid derived from the carbonization of coal, is classified into three market types based on temperature during production: high temperature, medium temperature, and low temperature coal tar. High temperature coal tar is produced at over 1,000°C, yielding a complex mixture ideal for producing chemicals and materials. Medium temperature coal tar is produced at 600-1,000°C, primarily used in industrial applications. Low temperature coal tar, obtained below 600°C, is often utilized in roofing and paving, known for its viscous properties.</p></p>
