<p><p>The animal parasite prevention and control market consists of three primary types: topical medication, injection medication, and oral medication. Topical medications are applied directly to the skin or coat to eliminate or repel parasites. Injection medications involve administering a dose via a syringe, offering long-lasting protection and rapid action against pests. Oral medications are ingested, providing systematic treatment to control internal and external parasites. Each method offers distinct advantages depending on the type of parasite and the animal species involved.</p></p>
