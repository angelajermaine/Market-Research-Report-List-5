<p><p>The blood cancer therapeutics market comprises various types, including Chronic Myeloid Leukemia (CML), characterized by the overproduction of myeloid cells, often treated with targeted therapies like tyrosine kinase inhibitors. Acute Lymphocytic Leukemia (ALL) affects lymphoid cells and requires aggressive treatment regimens, often including chemotherapy and immunotherapy. Chronic Lymphocytic Leukemia (CLL) is marked by the accumulation of B lymphocytes; therapies include monoclonal antibodies and targeted agents. Acute Myeloid Leukemia (AML) is a rapid progression of myeloid cells, commonly treated with intensive chemotherapy and novel therapies.</p></p>
