<p><p>The synthetic biodegradable polymer market includes various types, each with unique properties. Aliphatic polymers are flexible and degrade through hydrolysis. Poly glycoside acid (PGA) offers high tensile strength and biodegradability. Polylactic acid (PLA) is a widely used, bio-based polymer known for its versatility. Polycaprolactone (PCL) is elastomeric and suitable for slow degradation applications. Polyphosphoesters provide tunable properties due to their phosphate linkages, while polyanhydrides are used for drug delivery systems. Other materials in this category may include innovative copolymers and blends.</p></p>
