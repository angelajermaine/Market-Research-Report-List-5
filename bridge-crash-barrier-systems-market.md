<p><p>Bridge crash barrier systems are categorized into three main types: semi-rigid, rigid, and flexible barriers. Semi-rigid barriers combine features of both rigid and flexible designs, providing moderate energy absorption and deflection. Rigid barriers offer robust protection, typically made from concrete or steel, preventing vehicle penetration but may result in higher impact forces. Flexible barriers, often made from materials like wire rope, absorb energy through deformation, offering lower impact forces and allowing for some vehicle displacement, enhancing safety on bridges.</p></p>
