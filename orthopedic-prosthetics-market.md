<p><p>The orthopedic prosthetics market is divided into two main types: upper prosthesis and lower prosthesis. Upper prosthesis includes devices designed for amputations above the elbow or wrist, enhancing functionality in reaching, grasping, and daily activities. Lower prosthesis caters to limb loss below the hip or knee, focusing on mobility and support for walking, running, or other physical activities. Each segment addresses specific patient needs, utilizing advanced materials and technologies to improve user comfort and performance.</p></p>
