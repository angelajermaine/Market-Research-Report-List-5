<p><p>The natural food emulsifier market includes various types, each serving specific functions in food formulations. Lecithin, derived from soy or egg, stabilizes fats and improves texture. Lanolin, extracted from sheep's wool, acts as a thickening agent. Gum Arabic, a plant-derived gum, offers emulsification and improves mouthfeel. Other emulsifiers may include plant extracts and proteins, providing versatility for food manufacturers. These natural emulsifiers enhance product consistency, extend shelf life, and meet consumer demand for clean-label ingredients.</p></p>
