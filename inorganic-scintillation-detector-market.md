<p><p>The inorganic scintillation detector market consists of three primary types: solid, liquid, and gaseous scintillation detectors. Solid scintillation detectors use crystalline materials to convert ionizing radiation into detectable light, widely utilized in medical imaging and radiation monitoring. Liquid scintillation detectors employ liquid solvents to detect beta radiation, often used in environmental and biological applications. Gaseous scintillation detectors, utilizing gas-filled chambers, are suitable for detecting high-energy particle radiation and are commonly applied in research and industrial settings.</p></p>
