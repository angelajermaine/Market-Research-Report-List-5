<p><ul><li>Intraoral X-rays</li><li>Extraoral X-rays</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The dental X-ray market is categorized into two main types: intraoral and extraoral X-rays. Intraoral X-rays are taken within the mouth, providing detailed images of individual teeth, supporting diagnostics for cavities, root issues, and bone loss. Conversely, extraoral X-rays are captured outside the mouth, offering broader views of the teeth, jaw, and facial structure, often used for orthodontics and surgical planning. Both types are crucial for comprehensive oral health assessment and treatment planning.</p></p>
