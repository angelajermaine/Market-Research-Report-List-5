<p><p>Fluorescence Activated Cell Sorting (FACS) is a sophisticated laboratory technique that enables the separation of heterogeneous mixtures of cells based on their specific light scattering and fluorescent characteristics. Utilizing lasers and fluorescent dyes, FACS allows researchers to isolate and analyze individual cells with high precision, which is crucial for various applications in immunology, cancer research, and stem cell biology.</p><p>The global FACS market is exhibiting robust growth, anticipated to expand at a CAGR of 10.00% during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to several factors, including the rising prevalence of cancer and infectious diseases, increasing investment in cell-based research, and advancements in technology that enhance sorting efficiency and accuracy. Furthermore, the growing application of FACS in personalized medicine is driving demand, as it provides critical insights into cellular mechanisms.</p><p>Recent trends in the market include the integration of artificial intelligence and machine learning to optimize sorting processes and data analysis. Additionally, there is an increasing focus on miniaturization and automation of FACS systems, which enhances user-friendliness and accessibility. These developments are expected to further propel the FACS market forward, making it an essential tool in modern biomedical research.</p></p>
