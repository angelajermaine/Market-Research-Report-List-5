<p><p>The Photonics Integrated Circuit (IC) market consists of three primary integration types: Monolithic Integration, Hybrid Integration, and Module Integration. Monolithic Integration involves fabricating all photonic components on a single substrate, maximizing efficiency and reducing size. Hybrid Integration combines components made from different materials or processes, allowing for enhanced performance and flexibility. Module Integration refers to assembling various functional units into a coherent module, which can integrate both electronic and photonic functionalities, catering to diverse applications in communication, sensing, and data processing.</p></p>
