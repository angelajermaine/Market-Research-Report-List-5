<p><p>Contactless IC cards are categorized primarily into two types: memory cards and microprocessor cards. Memory cards store data but lack processing capability, making them simpler and typically used for applications like transit systems or loyalty programs. In contrast, microprocessor cards come with an embedded processor, enabling complex functions such as encryption, secure transactions, and data management. These are ideal for applications requiring higher security, such as banking and identification, allowing for versatile usage in various sectors.</p></p>
