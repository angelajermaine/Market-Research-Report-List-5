<p><p>The Medical Hygiene PP Non Woven Fabric Market is categorized into four primary types: drylaid, airlaid, wetlaid, and spunlaid. Drylaid involves bonding fibers using mechanical means, offering durability for medical applications. Airlaid utilizes air to distribute fibers, creating a soft, absorbent material ideal for hygiene products. Wetlaid involves a water-based process, producing smooth and uniform fabrics, while spunlaid combines spunbonding and meltblown techniques, resulting in strong, breathable nonwovens suitable for medical hygiene uses. Each type addresses specific performance and application needs in the healthcare sector.</p></p>
